Questions for the week:


  1. Question #1 Easy

    Given an array where every element occurs three times, except one element which occurs only once. Find the element that occurs once. Expected time complexity is O(n) and O(1) extra space.
    Examples:
    Input: arr[] = {10, 1, 10, 3, 10, 1, 1, 2, 3, 3}
    Output: 2

  2. Question #2 Easy

    Code a power function without using multiplication (*) and division (/) operators.
    Example:
    Input: 5, 4
    Output: 625

  3. Question #3 Medium

    Given 'n' rows, generate the first 'n' rows of Pascal’s triangle.
    For example,
    Input = 5
    Output
    1
    1,1
    1,2,1
    1,3,3,1
    1,4,6,4,1

  4. Question #4

    Imagine you are a middle school teacher, and you’re going into a class just after the maths period. The students were captivated with the greatness of numbers. This being the case you ask each students age, they reply their age in number of days. (Do not consider leap years)
    Write a C/C++ Program to calculate :

    • Easy

      • 1. The age of the student.

    • Medium

      • 2. The number of Years,Months,Weeks,Days the student has lived.


  5. Question #5

    Imagine you have an 8 year old little brother Kumaresh and you’ve lost his calculator. Kumaresh has to do his maths assignment which is due tomorrow. You have a computer, but the computer does not have an internet connection, also it doesn’t have a pre-installed calculator app. Fortunately for Kumaresh you are a computer science student.

    You are going to build a C/C++ Program to help your brother do his assignment. Your code should meet the following conditions:

    • Easy

      • 1. The operations your calculator should support are : ‘+’, ‘-’, ‘*’, ‘/’, ‘%’

    • Medium

      • 2. The operations your calculator should also support are : ‘^’, ‘√’

      3. Your app should not exit unless specified.

    • Hard

      • 4. It should be able to handle any number of inputs.

  6. Question #6 Hard

    Imagine you are the student representative of your class. Your faculty gives you a list of student names in your class, he/she asks you to create an attendance register for the class.

    Write a C/C++ Program to arrange the unordered list of names into an ordered list.

  7. Bonus Question (extra points for that extra edge!)
  8. Question #7 Hard (6 pts)

    Imagine you are the student representative of your class. Your PED gives you a list of student names with their heights in your class, he/she asks you to give him the height of the students one by one as he calls out the names.

    Write a C/C++ Program to print the height of the student for the name of that student being the input.

  9. Question #8 Hard (8 pts)

    Imagine you are the student representative of your class. Your PED gives you a list of student names with their heights in your class, he/she asks you to create a list in which the student names are arranged based on their heights.

    Write a C/C++ Program to print the names of the students based on their heights.

Points scoring scheme:

  • Easy == 1 point
  • Medium == 2 points
  • Hard == 4 points
